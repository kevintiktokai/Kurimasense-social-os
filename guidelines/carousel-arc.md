# Carousel Arc

A carousel is an episode with a shape. Design for the three actions that beat
the algorithm: **saves, shares, sends.** A post that gets sent to an agronomist
or a farming WhatsApp group is worth more than a hundred passive likes.

## The default arc (6–8 slides)
1. **Cover / hook** — name the felt pain + the promise. Stop the scroll.
   One idea. The hook line = frame-1 text = first caption line (see hooks).
2. **Stakes** — why it matters now, ideally one sourced number (data bank).
3–5. **The teach** — the method, in steps or signals. One idea per slide.
   This is the value; make each slide independently screenshot-worthy.
6. **Turn** — the "so what": what changes when you do this.
7. **CTA / question** — engineer the save & the send. A question the reader
   wants to answer, or a checklist worth keeping.

Not every carousel is 8 slides. The Playbook may be 6; By The Numbers may be
5. But every carousel has: a hook, at least one unit of real value, and a
close that asks for a save or a send.

## Slide-to-template mapping
| Arc beat | Template(s) |
|---|---|
| Cover / hook | `cover`, `hero` |
| Stakes | `case-study`, `insight` |
| Teach | `insight`, `listicle`, `comparison`, `icon-row` |
| Turn | `insight`, `feature` |
| CTA / question | `question` |

## Rules that make carousels get saved
- **One idea per slide.** If you need "and", split the slide.
- **Front-load value.** Slide 2 must already teach — never make slide 2 an
  "about us." Earn the swipe.
- **Every slide stands alone.** People screenshot slide 4, not the set. Each
  slide carries enough context to make sense on its own.
- **The last slide is a tool, not a goodbye.** A checklist to save, a question
  to answer, a thing to send to someone who needs it.
- **Number your teach.** "Signal 01 / 02 / 03" or "01 / 02 / 03" gives the
  reader a spine and a reason to reach the end.

## Sends are designed, not hoped for
Name the person to send to. "Send this to whoever makes your spray decisions."
"Forward to your agronomist before the next field walk." The most trusted
referral path in Zimbabwean farming is grower → agronomist and grower → grower;
write the send instruction for that path explicitly.

## The "why this works" note (law: explainability)
Every brief carries a `rationale`. Before a carousel is built, that note must
answer: what is the one idea? what's the felt pain? which arc beat is each
slide? why will it be saved or sent? If the note can't answer those, the
carousel isn't ready.
