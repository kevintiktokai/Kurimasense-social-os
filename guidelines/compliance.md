# Compliance — the truth rules

These are hard laws. A post that breaks one does not ship, regardless of how
good it looks. The critic pass (Phase 7) checks every one.

## 1. Never fabricate (Law 5)
No invented statistics, results, testimonials, clients, case studies, or
quotes. Ever.
- **Every number** comes from `intelligence/data-bank.json` with a source and
  year. If it's not in the bank, it doesn't go on a slide — full stop.
- **No fake clients or testimonials.** We have no "a farmer in Mash West saw
  +40%" unless that is a real, documented, permission-granted result in the
  bank. Until we have real customer results, use **sourced sector data** and
  clearly-labelled **modelled scenarios** instead.
- **Modelled ≠ measured.** A worked scenario must be labelled as illustrative
  ("modelled", "for illustration"), never presented as an observed outcome.

## 2. Source-on-surface
When a stat appears on a slide, its source + year appears too — on the
case-study source line, or in the first comment. The audience can always trace
a KurimaSense number to its origin. This is a feature, not fine print.

## 3. No overclaiming the product
- KurimaSense provides **decision support from evidence** — it does not
  "guarantee yield", "eliminate risk", or "predict the future".
- Satellite/model outputs are **indications to act on**, confirmed on the
  ground. Never imply the score replaces the farmer's judgement or an
  agronomist's visit; it directs them.
- Ranges and uncertainty are stated honestly ("stress *likely* in the NE
  corner"), not laundered into false precision.

## 4. Data-bank confidence discipline
- `high` confidence → usable as a headline stat.
- `medium` → usable, phrase as "studies/estimates suggest".
- `context` → directional only; never the spine of a claim, never a headline
  number. (e.g. the +300% irrigation case is an upper bound, not a promise.)
- Re-verify anything older than 3 years before headline use; drought and price
  figures decay fastest.

## 5. Imagery & brand safety (ties to Phase 6)
- No public figures, no identifiable individuals as implied endorsers.
- No copyrighted or trademarked material (brands, logos — watch stock photos
  for machinery livery).
- Imagery abstract/anonymous; dignified, never poverty-porn.

## 6. Agronomic responsibility
- No blanket agronomic instructions that could harm if wrong ("spray X at rate
  Y") without "confirm for your crop, stage and region / consult your
  agronomist". We teach *how to decide*, not prescriptions that ignore context.
- Chemical/input advice stays at the level of method and timing, not specific
  dose recommendations, unless sourced and caveated.

## 7. Market & dignity laws
British/SA spelling, USD, no saviour framing (see `market.md`). These are
compliance issues, not style preferences.

## The critic's checklist (Phase 7 runs this)
- [ ] Every number traces to the data bank (id cited in brief).
- [ ] Source + year on-surface for each stat used.
- [ ] No testimonial/client/result that isn't real & documented.
- [ ] Modelled scenarios labelled as such.
- [ ] No product overclaim; uncertainty stated honestly.
- [ ] No banned frames or US-defaults.
- [ ] Imagery brand-safe (no figures, no copyrighted marks).
- [ ] One idea; hook matches caption line 1 matches frame 1.
