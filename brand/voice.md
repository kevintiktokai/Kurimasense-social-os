# KurimaSense — Brand Voice

## Who we are
KurimaSense sells the end of farming on guesswork. We turn satellite field
intelligence — a KurimaScore per field, stress alerts, yield models — into
plain calls a farmer can act on this week. *Kurima* is Shona for "to farm";
*sense* is what we add to it.

## Who we speak to
One person at a time:
- **The commercial grower / farm manager** running maize, tobacco, soya,
  horticulture — juggling inputs, labour and weather on thin margins.
- **The smallholder** in Natural Regions III–V, mostly rain-fed, deciding
  where scarce fertiliser and effort should go.
- **The agronomist / Agritex officer** advising many fields at once.
- **The agribusiness / offtaker / lender** carrying portfolio risk across
  hundreds of growers.

Write to the grower by default. When a post is for advisors or institutions,
say so in the brief — but never talk down to the farmer to reach them.

## The enemy
Farming blind: planting, spraying and selling on guesswork, then finding out
at harvest what went wrong — when it is too late and too expensive to fix.
Every post is, somewhere, about replacing a guess with evidence.

## Voice in five words
Direct. Senior. Evidence-led. Anti-hype. Warm.

## How that sounds
- **Educator first, builder second.** Teach something usable even if the
  reader never buys. 80% of posts teach with no product mention.
- **Speak to one person.** "Your block," not "farmers should."
- **Evidence over adjectives.** A sourced number beats "revolutionary."
  If we can't source it, we don't say it (see `guidelines/compliance.md`).
- **Plain, senior register.** Short sentences. No jargon we don't unpack.
  We sound like the best agronomist you know, not a brochure.
- **Anti-hype.** No "game-changer", "unlock", "revolutionise", "cutting-edge",
  "AI-powered magic". No emoji in body copy. No hustle-culture urgency.
- **Confident, not boastful.** We show the method; we don't crow.

## Market register (law — see `guidelines/market.md`)
- British / SA spelling: *fertiliser, maize, tonne, colour, programme, realise.*
- Currency in **USD** (the working currency of Zimbabwean agriculture).
- Local furniture, used correctly: seasons (the rains, first planting rains,
  tasselling), crops (maize, tobacco, soya, groundnuts, small grains),
  institutions (Agritex, GMB, TIMB, mukando/rounds), rhythms (market days).
- **Never** the saviour frame. Not "bringing tech to Africa," not "empowering
  poor farmers." Our audience are experts in their own operation; we hand them
  a sharper instrument, nothing more.

## We say / we don't say
| We say | We don't say |
|---|---|
| "Your maize is running warm in Block B." | "Revolutionary AI detects crop stress!" |
| "Three checks before you spray." | "Unlock next-level yields 🚀" |
| "Post-harvest loss runs ~20% of cereals (FAO)." | "You're losing a fortune!" (unsourced) |
| "Scout the hotspot, not the whole field." | "Our platform empowers African farmers." |
| "Priced in USD. Built for the rains." | "Bringing Silicon Valley to the bush." |

## Sentence rhythm
Open with the reader's reality. Land one idea. Close with a next action or a
question. One idea per post — if a slide needs "and also," it's two posts.

## Signature moves
- Name a felt cost in time or money ("two weeks too late", "at harvest").
- Second person, present tense.
- One sourced number as the spine of any claim of stakes.
- End teaching posts on a question or a save-worthy checklist.
